Neural networks are conventionally thought to have limited interpretability, especially within the geosciences. We use tools developed by the computer science community, such as layer-wise relevance propagation, to understand how and why neural networks make their predictions.

We focus on two objectives: building trust in machine learning models when their decisions align with physical principles, and using neural networks to uncover new insights about Earth systems.
