We build neural networks to forecast and represent the Earth system, then study what they learned — which regions, variables, and relationships drive a prediction — to find sources of predictability we can test against physical understanding. A model that predicts well has, in effect, proposed a hypothesis about how the system works. Our job is to read that hypothesis back out and check it.

In practice:

- Interpretable architectures whose internal logic maps onto physical quantities
- Attribution and feature-importance methods applied to climate and weather fields
- Turning explanations into testable claims about the Earth system, not post-hoc stories
