The climate system is notoriously difficult to predict several weeks to multiple years into the future. Across these subseasonal-to-decadal ("S2D") timescales, predictions are both too far into the future for weather models to show skill, and too short for climate model projections due to noisy climate variability.

We examine predictable climate modes — including the Madden–Julian Oscillation, El Niño–Southern Oscillation, and Pacific Decadal Oscillation — to identify periods with enhanced predictability, sometimes called "forecasts of opportunity."
